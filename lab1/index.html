How to Clean a Dog Kennel

By: Sarah Ricks

Step 1: Gather all the tools you need

Before you begin cleaning you will need to gather all the supplies you need to complete the task. All the supplies are located in the dog kennel room, if extra supplies are needed they are located in the laundry room.

The supplies you will need are:

  • Dog treats
  • Protective gloves
  • Hose-end foamer and hose or spray bottle
  • Cleaner -concentrated accelerated H202
  • Scoop tools
  • Squeegee
  • Scrub brush
  • Clean food and water bowls
  • Clean bedding
  • Clean toys for enrichment
  • Trash bin or poop scoop pin

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.


Step 2: Move dogs to outside kennels

Using a loop leash, move the dogs from the indoor kennel to the outdoor kennel, one dog at a time.

To properly move the dog you should:

  • Wait until dog is sitting at kennel door
  • Use positive reinforcement by giving dog treats when desired behavior is achieved
  • Once dog is sitting, slowly open the kennel door, blocking the opening with your legs
  • Gently slip the loop leash around the dogs neck and secure the leash
  • Let the dog out of the kennel and close kennel door
  • Walk the dog to the outside kennel
  • Open the outside kennel door and walk the dog inside
  • Once the dog settles gently loosen the loop leash and slip off the dog's neck, while blocking the open door with your legs
  • Then close the kennel door and lock it

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 3: Remove all items from the kennels

Remove all the bowls, blankets, bedding and toys from the dog kennel.

When removing items from the kennel make sure to:

  • Put on protective gloves
  • Put soiled bedding and blankets in the laundry bins located in the laundry room
  • Put used toys in the bin to be washed and sanitized in the laundry room
  • Put dirty bowls in the dish washer in the laundry room

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 4: Scoop up feces and debris

Once kennels are empty, scoop up any feces or debris from the kennel using scooping tools.

  • Scooping tools are located in each kennel room against the back wall
  • Put the feces or debris in the trash bin/poop scooping bin

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 5: Soak kennel floor with cleaning solution

Once debris is removed, use scrub brush and mop bucket to soak the kennel floor with cleaning solution.

Steps to prepare cleaning solution:

  • Retrieve the mop bucket and scrub brush
  • Fill bucket with water
  • Dilute the solution properly with bleach
  • For every 1 part bleach use 32 parts water

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 6: Scrub floors and walls with cleaning solution

Use the long-handled scrub brush and mop bucket to scrub the kennel walls and floors thoroughly with cleaning solution.

Tips for scrubbing the kennels:

  • Start by scrubing the walls of the kennel first
  • Start at the back of the kennel and work your way forward toward door
  • Make sure to scrub floors thoroughly to remove excess or stuck-on debris
  • Make sure to scrub the platform and lift to scrub underneith

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 7: Let disinfectant stand for five minutes

It is important to let the cleaning solution stand for five minutes so it can fully disinfect the kennel to prevent the spread of disease.

While you wait you should move on to the next kennel and start soaking it with the bleach water cleaning solution and then scrubbing it using the same steps as above.

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 8: Hose down the kennel to rinse off cleaning solution

Turn on the hose located in the kennel room and spray down the kennel to to rinse off the bleach and water cleaning solution.

Tips for spraying down the kennel:

  • Start by spraying the walls of the kennel first
  • Then spray the front of the kennel first working your way back towards the drain
  • When done spraying make sure to turn off the hose
  • Make sure to roll the hose back up once finished spraying the kennels

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 9: Squeegee the kennel

Using the squeegee located on the back wall of the kennel room, push the excess water down the drain.

Tips for squeegeeing the kennel:

  • Start by squeegeeing the front of the kennel first working your way back towards the drain
  • Make sure to get all the excess water down the drain
  • Then let the kennel air dry before moving on to the next step
  • While waiting for kennel to dry, start squeegeeing the other kennels

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 10: Put clean items in the kennel

Replace all the items in the kennel with clean ones from the laundry room.

Items needed for each kennel:

  • Clean bedding and blankets
  • Clean toys for enrichment
  • Clean bowls for food and water
  • Water! Make sure to refill water bowl. No need to fill food bowl, the second feeding will come later in the day

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

Step 11: Return dog to clean kennel

Grab a loop leash and treats, then go retrieve the dog from the outside kennel and bring the dog back to the clean kennel

To properly move the dog you should:

  • Wait until dog is sitting at the outside kennel door
  • Use positive reinforcement by giving dog treats when desired behavior is achieved
  • Once dog is sitting, slowly open the kennel door, blocking the opening with your legs
  • Gently slip the loop leash around the dogs neck and secure the leash
  • Let the dog out of the kennel and close kennel door
  • Walk the dog to the now clean inside kennel
  • Open the inside kennel door and walk the dog inside
  • Once the dog settles gently loosen the loop leash and slip off the dog's neck, while blocking the open door with your legs
  • Then close the kennel door and lock it

Photo provided by the ICHS staff.

That's all the cleaning steps!

Once done with the first dog kennel cleaning, make sure to repeat all the above steps until all kennels are clean. Always start by cleaning the quarantine kennels first and then move on to the regular dog kennels.
